Pressure Review: Peruzzi and Fireboy DML Take Their Collaborations to the Next Level

Pressure is a second collaborative track from Peruzzi and Fireboy DML, following their successful debut release, Southy Love, in 2021. Much like their previous effort, Pressure delivers an undeniable appeal, blending the best elements of Amapiano and Dancehall but with a twist that elevates it to a whole new level.

This time around, the song features Wande Coal‘s distinct vocal style and attitude in the Fuji-centric verses, adding an unexpected but welcome dimension to the track. It’s no wonder that Fireboy DML, who instinctively sounds like Wande Coal, was able to channel the stylistic character of one of his idols with such ease, making for a genuinely authentic vocal performance.

The bouncy Amapiano beats and Dancehall tempo create a vibrant and energetic atmosphere, with entrapping vocal fillers, distant artificial choir sounds, and electronic guitar notes contributing to its explosive nature. From the first listen, Pressure commands your attention, leaving an impression that lingers long after the song has ended.

But beyond its infectious rhythm and catchy chorus, Pressure is a testament to the incredible talent of these artists. Peruzzi‘s and Fireboy DML‘s vocal abilities are on full display, their voices complementing each other perfectly, while Wande Coal‘s addition adds an unexpected yet delightful twist to the song.

The artists have once again delivered a hit that showcases their unique musical talents and highlights the best of their respective genres, setting the bar high for future collaborations. All in all, Pressure is a dazzling display of musical collaboration that will leave you wanting more. Its energy and authenticity make it a standout track, deserving a well-earned rating of 8.1/10.
