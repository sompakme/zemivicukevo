Calling All Spartan Superfans! Can You Pass This Crucial MSU Trivia?

Hey there, Green and White warriors! Feeling confident in your Michigan State knowledge? Let's put that fandom to the test with a question that might stump even the most die-hard Spartan: In what year did Michigan State make its grand entrance onto the College Football Playoff (CFP) stage?

For those who haven't been glued to the gridiron the past decade, the CFP is the current playoff system that determines the national champion in college football. It's basically the Super Bowl of the sport, with the best of the best battling it out for ultimate glory.

So, when did the Spartans join this elite group?

Drumroll, please...

The answer is... 2015! That's right, folks! Back in that glorious year, the Spartans, led by the ever-intense Coach Dantonio, put together a season for the ages. They clawed their way through the Big Ten like a wolverine with a sweet tooth, and ultimately secured a spot in the CFP.

A Night to Remember: The Cotton Bowl Classic

The CFP appearance culminated in a heart-stopping Cotton Bowl Classic against the powerhouse Alabama Crimson Tide. While the final score wasn't quite what Spartan Nation craved (ahem, Alabama 38, Michigan State 0), it was a historic moment nonetheless. The Spartans proved they belonged among the college football giants!

Did You Know? Fun Facts About MSU's CFP Run

  • Spartans entered the CFP ranked a respectable No. 3.
  • Their journey wasn't without its drama. A thrilling last-second field goal secured them the Big Ten East title!
  • While they may not have taken home the championship trophy, the Spartans still finished the season with a solid 12-2 record.
