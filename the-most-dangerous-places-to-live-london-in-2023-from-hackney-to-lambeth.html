The most dangerous places to live London in 2023 from Hackney to Lambeth

The latest figures from the Metropolitan Police show where the most dangerous boroughs to live in London are. Offences range from sexual assaults to knife crime and even theft.

In January 2023 there were 21,522 reports of theft alone which was the most common offence. Overall there were 82,453 offences reported in the Monthly Crime Data statistics.

This works our as just over 9 offences per 1,000 of the London population. Overall, crime rates have risen 6.4 per cent in the past year.

MyLondon has organised the top 10 London boroughs per borough population to show where the most dangerous boroughs in the capital are. (Note- Homicide and domestic abuse are not included in these totals).

10. Hackney - 9.8 offences per 1,000

The East London borough has gone through many changes in recent times. The area is still a very sort after location in London but house prices have risen substantially over the past couple of years.

Overall in Hackney there were 2,753 offences reported in Hackney in January. Of these theft was most prominent followed by violence against the person and vehicle offences.

9. Lambeth - 9.8 offences per 1,000

Lambeth saw 3,163 offences committed in January, the most being theft which made up 870 of the offences committed. After theft violence against the person was the next most common crime with 829 offences committed.

8. Tower Hamlets - 10.1 offences per 1,000

Out of the 3,338 offences in Tower Hamlets in January, 831 were thefts. Next on the list was violence against the person which saw 793 recorded offences, followed by vehicle offences where 348 were recorded.

7. Southwark - 10.1 offences per 1,000

For Southwark the picture is the same as most of the top 10 boroughs with theft being the most prominent offence committed. Out of the 3,257 total offences, 961 were theft, 686 were violence against the person and 324 were put down as other accepted crimes (crimes that usually are dealt with in a magistrates court opposed to criminal court).

6. Islington - 10.4 offences per 1,000

The North London borough of Islington was sixth on the list. They had 2,570 offences recorded and also had theft at the top with 785 reported. As like Southwark, violence against the person was second with 561 offences and 205 recorded as other accepted crimes.

5. Hammersmith and Fulham - 10.4 offences per 1,000

Hammersmith and Fulham had 1,907 recorded offences in January 2023. Being one of the least populous boroughs in London according to the 2021 census, means that it features high on the list despite recording one of the lowest total number of offences.

Here the most recorded offence was violence against the person which saw 469 offences recorded. After came theft with 456 and vehicle offences with 269.

4. Haringey - 10.7 offences per 1,000

For Haringey the recorded crimes is much the same as Hammersmith and Fulham. It had 2,841 offences recorded with 632 of those from violence, 581 from theft and then 393 were vehicle offences.

3. Camden - 11.7 offences per 1,000

Camden has a big tourist and nightlife scene which is why it is said to be high on lists of recorded crimes. Out of the 3190 offences recorded, 1,325 were thefts which is the second highest number of thefts in the city. Second in Camden was violence against the person with 588 recorded, followed by vehicle offences at 252 recorded.

2. Kensington and Chelsea - 11.9 offences per 1,000

The richest borough in London in terms of high earners is Kensington and Chelsea. It is also the least populous borough in the capital (not including City of London) and therefore features highly on the list despite having the lowest recorded offences in the top 10 at 1,847. The most common offence committed here was Theft at 632 with violence against the person next at 322.

1. Westminster - 29.2 offences per 1,000

Westminster is by far the most dangerous borough for crimes per 1,000 of its population. Because parts of it are in the city, the footfall in this borough is high. There were 8104 offences reported in January 2023 in Westminster, which is over double the closest borough to it. Of these offences 4860 were theft, with violence next at 970 and vehicle offences after at 398.
