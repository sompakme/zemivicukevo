Physical therapy school sounds like the perfect path, but before you dive headfirst into studying kinesiology (that's the fancy word for muscle movement, for those not in the know), let's talk about the hefty price tag that comes with this noble profession.

Don't Despair, PT Hopeful! Here's How to Ease the Financial Burden

So, the price tag might be enough to make you wince, but don't despair! There are ways to soften the blow:

  • Scholarship Shuffle: Get busy with scholarship applications! There are scholarships specifically for PT students, so put those essay-writing skills to good use.

  • Loan Ranger: Student loans are a common option, but make sure you understand the terms and interest rates before you sign on the dotted line.

Remember, this is an investment in your future career! While the cost stings now, the earning potential for PTs in Georgia can make this investment worthwhile.

PT School in Georgia: FAQ

How to get into PT school in Georgia?

Most programs require a bachelor's degree, strong science grades, and some volunteer experience in physical therapy settings. Check individual program requirements for specifics.

How long is PT school in Georgia?

PT programs are typically full-time, three-year doctoral programs.

How much do PTs make in Georgia?

The average salary for PTs in Georgia is around $85,000 per year (source: [salary info for PTs in GA]).

How to pay for PT school in Georgia?

Financial aid options include scholarships, grants, and student loans. Explore all options to find the best fit for your situation.

How to become a PT in Georgia?

After graduating from an accredited PT program, you'll need to pass the National Physical Therapy Exam (NPTE) and obtain a state license.
