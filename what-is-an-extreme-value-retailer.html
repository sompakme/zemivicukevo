What does a wholesaler look like in practise?

Probably the most well-known example of a wholesaler is a corporation that acquires finished items from manufacturers and then distributes these products to retailers, who in turn sell lesser amounts of the product to end users. Some wholesalers may also purchase from or sell to other wholesalers in addition to their own.

What exactly is the distinction between a wholesaler and a retailer?

Wholesale simply refers to the sale of products in large numbers, while retail refers to the sale of merchandise in small amounts. While a wholesaler sells items to companies, they also acquire things in order to resell them to other businesses. A retailer, on the other hand, is someone who sells items directly to the ultimate customer.

What exactly is a category killer in the marketing world?

A category killer is a retailer that focuses in and sells a broad product range within a certain category, and who, via product selection, price, and market penetration, achieves a significant competitive advantage over other shops in that area. Category killers include retailers such as Barnes & Noble, Best Buy, and Staples, to name a few examples.
