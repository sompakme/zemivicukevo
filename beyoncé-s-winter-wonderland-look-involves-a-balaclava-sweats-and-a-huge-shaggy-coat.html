Beyonc's Winter Wonderland Look Involves a Balaclava, Sweats, and a Huge Shaggy Coat

Only Beyoncé could make sweatpants look this luxe.

The “Alien Superstar” singer recently dropped photos on Instagram of a slick new monochromatic outfit—she built the perfect winter wonderland look via mismatching white pieces.

These included a white Alaïa crop top with a hooded balaclava attachment, which she wore over her head to frame wavy, wispy strands of her platinum blonde hair. For her bottoms, Beyoncé opted for ivory sweatpants with a straight-leg silhouette from Wardrobe.Nyc. The singer kept warm in a slouchy single-breasted blazer, also from Wardrobe.Nyc, layered underneath a huge shaggy white fur coat, then topped off the look with white pointed-toe boots, black sunglasses, and a small stainless steel box bag by Brandon Blackwood.

Some photos show Jay-Z posing with his wife in his own monochromatic look, although he went the opposite direction: The rapper wore a black V-neck ribbed-knit sweater, black pants with a thin white line running down the sides, a long black coat, a black beanie, black shades, and white sneakers.

Beyoncé, who recently wrapped up her sold-out Renaissance World Tour, celebrated her forthcoming film from the concert this past weekend. For the star-studded premiere on Saturday night, she wore a metallic silver strapless gown custom-made by Versace. The jaw-dropping dress came with on-theme accessories, including silver skintight opera gloves and silver open-toe heels. Renaissance: A Film by Beyoncé will open in theaters this Friday, December 1.

The Grammy winner first teased her Renaissance album in an interview for Harper’s Bazaar’s September 2021 cover story. “I feel a renaissance emerging, and I want to be part of nurturing that escape in any way possible,” she said at the time. “I’ve been in the studio for a year and a half. Sometimes it takes a year for me to personally search through thousands of sounds to find just the right kick or snare. One chorus can have up to 200 stacked harmonies. Still, there’s nothing like the amount of love, passion, and healing that I feel in the recording studio.”
