Early Life and Career Beginnings

Sheryl Underwood was born on October 28, 1963, in Little Rock, Arkansas. She had a challenging childhood, but she used her experiences to fuel her comedy. She began her career in the entertainment industry as a stand-up comedian in the late 1980s. Her unique style and humor quickly gained her recognition, and she started making appearances on television shows and comedy specials.

Rise to Fame

Underwood’s big break came in 1992 when she won the Miller Lite Comedy Search. This win catapulted her career, and she started getting more significant roles in television and film. She also became a sought-after comedian for various comedy tours and festivals.

Television Career

Sheryl Underwood’s television career took off in the late 1990s. She made appearances on shows like “The Steve Harvey Show” and “Hollywood Squares.” However, her most notable role came in 2011 when she joined the panel of the CBS daytime talk show, “The Talk.”

The Talk
